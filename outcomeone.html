1.1 Describing the purpose of IT in modern business

Information Technology (IT) is extremely important for all businesses to meet their day to day operational tasks and establish a secure and hassle-free communication flow. Combining IT with marketing and sales helps extend the businesses reach as well maintain fantastic customer relations. 1.2 Describing methods, skills and resources needed to complete digital projects successfully – what do you need to make projects successful? What tools? What skills?

What is a digital project? A digital project comprises the planning, preparation, and production that goes into creating electronic publications. However, nowadays it can be almost anything. A software application, a website, an online campaign, etc. What is true for digital projects is that they're projects delivered as a service to the client. In terms of the overall digital project, the digital project manager should employ a methodology to their plan. KANBAN for example: Which is a visual system for managing your project work as it moves through a process.

The main project plan processes could be broken down into the following areas:

    Understand the scope - it is important first to establish the objectives of the project, which may include a budget, delivery date and goals.

    The team - whose members usually belong to different groups and have different functions and tasks assigned by the project manager.

    The plan - A document or chart that encompassess all the steps of the digital project.

    Management of the project - An essential element that keeps the project on time and within budget.

    Communication - A valuable skill and method that enables you to communicate effectively on the project with your client, team, and other stakeholders.

    Time management - This can flex and change and is a key aspect to managing the project.

    Deadlines - Embedded within the document or chart. Outlines timelines for tasks.

With all digital projects the methods, skills and resources will be outlined in the overall project plan.

1.4 Describe the risks that might impact digital projects – what causes digital projects to fail? Why?

No project is immune to failure. Lack of planning or poor planning can easily lead your project to failure. Spike Cavell's survey also revealed that 40% of projects fail due to poor planning and lack of resources. Spend time making a solid plan for your project and it will help you in executing each phase of the project smoothly.

The most common reasons given for digital projects to fail were:

    Short term priorities taking over.

    The initial scope was poorly defined (and the initial strategy / vision was unclear).

    Trying to do too much work all at once.

    Silos between marketing and IT[2]

1.7 Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects – How does GDPR impact digital projects?

This new data protection regulation puts the consumer in the driver’s seat, and the task of complying with this regulation falls upon businesses and organizations. Otherwise, you’re failing to comply. What falls under GDPR compliance?

Well, GDPR applies to all businesses and organizations established in the EU, regardless of whether the data processing takes place in the EU or not. Even non-EU established organizations will be subject to GDPR. If your business offers goods and/ or services to citizens in the EU, then it’s subject to GDPR. All organizations and companies that work with personal data should appoint a data protection officer or data controller who is in charge of GDPR compliance. There are tough penalties for those companies and organizations who don’t comply with GDPR fines of up to 4% of annual global revenue or 20 million Euros, whichever is greater. How serious is the EU taking GDPR? Extremely seriously.

For example, British Airways faced an eye-watering fine that amounted to hundreds of millions or euros for failing to comply.

Now, many people might think that the GDPR is just an IT issue, but that is the furthest from the truth. It has broad-sweeping implications for the whole company, including the way companies handle marketing and sales activities.
